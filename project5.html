Medical Device Localization in Chest X-Rays

Developed a machine learning pipeline to localize medical devices in chest x-rays with 97% accuracy across diverse test sets. This project combines a Multi-Label Classifier with a YOLOv5 model to detect and generate bounding boxes around medical devices, ensuring robust performance in varied clinical imaging scenarios.

Project Overview

The objective of this project was to enhance the detection and localization of medical devices within chest x-rays. This is crucial for clinical diagnostics and effective patient care. By leveraging advanced machine learning techniques, the system not only classifies but also pinpoints the exact location of devices, streamlining the review process for radiologists.

Key Features

  • 97% detection accuracy across multiple datasets
  • Combines a Multi-Label Classifier with YOLOv5 for precise localization
  • Generates bounding boxes around detected medical devices
  • Adaptable to various imaging conditions and sources

Technical Details

The pipeline starts with a Multi-Label Classifier that pre-screens the x-ray images to identify potential areas of interest. Following this, the YOLOv5 model is employed to accurately localize and draw bounding boxes around the detected devices. This dual-model approach ensures that the system can handle the variability in chest x-ray images while maintaining a high level of precision and reliability.
